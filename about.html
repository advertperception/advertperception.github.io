RP Proposal - Patrick Mitchell

 

To what extent does the chosen soundtrack to an advertisement affect viewer perception?

- The question was chosen to explore the effect that music has on the way humans process and interpret visual media and how it can change based on the mood, style, genre and tempo of music.

 

Background research

In the report: Jon D. Morris and Mary Anne Boone (1998) ,”The Effects of Music on Emotional Response, Brand Attitude, and Purchase Intent in an Emotional Advertising Condition”[1] it is stated that music has a large part to play in the way a viewer perceives an advertisement. Through multiple tests they found that sombre/sad music had a greater affect on the test subjects. Format

My report will be in the form of a multi-modal website presentation that will show all my findings in an easy to access public way. I wish for my findings to be accessible to marketing teams to better understand how to make their advertisement stand out and for the receivers of the advertisements so they understand how they are influenced by advertisers choices.

 

Methodology

I will be using a combination of interviews; both in person and on the phone, and surveying large groups of people via a platform such as Google surveys or survey monkey. Along with this primary data I will also be looking at secondary sources in the forms of other institutions and researchers papers through Google scholar and the institution’s websites as well as information sourced from the Flinders University library.

 

Capability

The Research Project will be focusing on the information and communication capability. To me, this capability means understanding the way we as humans interpret, process, understand, and react to information. My question relates to this as the way we perceive audio visual clues is vital to understanding the way we interpret information, communicate with each-other, and use technology in advertising
